Ageless Alliance Website Redesign

Ageless Alliance is a non-profit organization that advocates against elder abuse through spreading awareness and pushing for policy changes.

As UX/UI designers, we have identified several points for improvement on their website.

We conducted user research, competitor analysis, created a user persona and a user flow, wireframed, and iterated on our design.

Through renovating Ageless Alliance’s website for a user-friendly interface focusing on sharing stories and making donations, we have improved their user experience.

The Problem

At first glance, we thought the website is confusing and not convenient for users to navigate.

There were allignment issues, broken links, color accesbililty issues, and hierarchy problems.

We wanted to know if users shared the same problem.

User Research/Testing

To understand the problems users may have with Ageless Alliance website, we conducted 7 online interviews on people between 25 - 40 years old.

During these interviews, we asked our users to navigate through the original website and answer questions focused on these key objectives on the right:

Can users understand the purpose of this website?

What are the pain points for our users to find resources through this website?

How might we make the website easier to navigate?

What frustrations do users encounter when trying to donate?

Key Findings

Our users think ...

The original aesthetic of the page is bland and often has too much text on a single page.
The navigation from page to page is confusing as some key elements are either unclear or not working.
Sympathy, personal connections, and goals of NPOs determine if users want to make donations.
Most think seniors are not tech-savvy.
Donation is one of the most important function in an NPO’s webpage.

We wanted to see how other sites of the same audience look.

Competitor Analysis

To understand what other elder-related foundations did for their users, we evaluated the websites of four competitors.

Elder Justice Coalition

Strengths

Very well laid out website, its clean and easy to read, easy to navigate.

Weaknesses

Lot of empty white space in the home page
No footer
Most pages are just plain with links
The search button appears at a weird spot.

Elders and Courts

Strengths

Visually engaging homepage design
Responsive top navigation

Weaknesses

Not clear of the purpose of the website
Text-heavy primary pages

The National Clearinghouse on Abuse in Later Life

Strengths

Clean web page layout
Easy-to-read, consistent fonts
Clear call-to-action buttons

Weaknesses

Dropdowns in the secondary pages contain a lot of text and is not visually appealing.
The website could use more pictures

National Association of Area Agencies on Aging

Strengths

Navigation is clear, tabs and side menus make it easy to get to pages.
Follows a clean design layout

Weaknesses

Overall, it's an overabundance of information, could be more concise and clear.
Looks a bit outdated, and the fonts could be a bit bigger and easier to read.

Evelyn’s Journey

We created a storyboard for Evelyn to better understand her emotions while interacting with Ageless Alliance.

We determined an optimal user flow for Evelyn.

User Flow

From the research listed above, we decided on a user flow for Evelyn. The flow mainly consists of 4 sections:
Knowing more about the NPO, Account creation and story sharing, Making a donation, and Spreading Awareness.

Card Sorting

To make sure Evelyn can easily go through the optimal user flow, we performed card sorting on the original website’s navigation system.

We aim to simplify the navigation to reduce redundant links that caused confusion and highlight Donation & Stories pages.

We started wireframing with our findings in mind.

Early Versions of Prototypes

We headed on to wireframe several versions of prototypes.
One of our goals was to invoke empathy with emotional images.
We constantly seeked feedbacks from more users as we continued to make improvements.
Here is an example of one of our early-stage design changing process.

Version 1

Hero image with navigation laid on top.

Users thought the navigation bar is blocking the hero image and that stories shoud be the main focus of the site.

Version 2

New Navigation bar, carousel banner. Moved stories before news.

Users thought the navigation bar lacked hierarchy and the carousel was distracting.

Version 3

Improved navigation bar (closer to our final design), hero image with cards laid at its bottom.

Key Findings

Our users think ...

Text font, size, positioning consistency issues.
Donation flow/interaction needs redesigning.
Navigation issues (Back buttons, navigation bar, “preserve scroll position”)
Hierarchy issues with pictures, text, and call-to-action buttons.
